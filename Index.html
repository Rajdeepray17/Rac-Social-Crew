About Our Club

Discover who we are and what drives our mission to serve the community

Building Tomorrow's Leaders

Rotaract is a global network of 18-30 year olds whose aim is to create positive change in communities and in themselves. The Rotaract Club of Social Crew is dedicated to building a better tomorrow through service above self.

We believe in the power of young people to make a difference. Through our various initiatives, we focus on community development, leadership training, and creating lasting friendships that span across cultures and borders.

Our club is part of Rotary International, the world's first service organization, giving us access to a global network of like-minded individuals committed to creating positive change.

Mahadan 10.0

Overview

Rotaract club of Social Crew undertook a significant initiative during Mahadan Week aimed at promoting voluntary blood donation and community engagement in Jamshedpur, Jharkhand. Over the course of the event, we successfully hosted nine blood donation camps, reflecting the commitment of our members and volunteers to this noble cause. Alongside the donation camps, we also organized creative activities such as poster-making competitions to raise awareness about blood donation and its crucial role in saving lives. This multifaceted approach ensured active participation from diverse groups, generating enthusiasm and emphasizing the importance of social responsibility. Our outstanding efforts culminated in receiving the Platinum Award from Mahadan for organizing the maximum number of camps, as well as recognition for collecting the highest number of blood units in our district RID 3250.


Agenda:

he central agenda of our project was to embody the spirit of Mahadan by encouraging regular voluntary blood donation to create a sustainable blood supply for patients in need. Mahadan‚Äôs mission highlights the importance of community participation in this life-saving activity, and through our camps and awareness drives, we aimed to break myths, inspire donors, and build a culture of altruism. Supplementing blood camps with awareness events, including creative competitions, helped us engage the youth and educate participants about the critical impact of blood donation on healthcare. Ultimately, our agenda was to foster a sense of solidarity, promote health consciousness, and contribute meaningfully to community well-being.


Social Impact

The impact of our initiative went beyond just the volume of blood collected; it strengthened community bonds and heightened collective awareness about the significance of blood donation. By achieving the highest collection figures in our district, we directly supported local hospitals and emergency care units, potentially saving hundreds of lives. The awareness programs and creative competitions stimulated broader engagement, educating individuals about the vital role they can play through simple voluntary acts. Our project set a benchmark for other clubs within RID 3250, inspiring them to increase their blood donation efforts. This ripple effect contributes to building a healthier and more compassionate society, driving long-term positive change in community health infrastructure.
